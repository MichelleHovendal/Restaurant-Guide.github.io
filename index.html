About this webpage

This webpage is made by Ellen Winther Mortensen, Julius Pulvertaft Rasmussen, and Michelle Siw Hovendal and is part of the final project in the course Social Data Analysis and Visualization at Technical University of Denmark (DTU). This webpage is made in spring 2021.

The aim of the webpage is about restaurants placed in North America, the data is based on yelp data. The Python explainer notebook is attached here.

Yelp
  [draft]

What is Yelp? Yelp is an American company founded in 2004. The idea behind the company is to publish crowd-sourced reviews about businesses through their website Yelp.com and mobile app Yelp. In the second quarter of 2019, Yelp has an average of 61.8 million unique visitors through their website, while it was 76.7 million unique visitors on the app. As the internet gets filled with more and more information and the likelihood of the information being false, Yelp then provides a local platform for people to discover the true information....

Exploratory Data Analysis
  [draft]

Before you get into the root cause analysis of this project, an exploratory data analysis is presented, which will give you an overview of different metrics used. The first section will give you a brief introduction to where the states, how many restaurants, but also present how many there are under the two self-made categories, namely kitchen and type. The next section will give an overview of how the stars are distributed on state, kitchen and type....
